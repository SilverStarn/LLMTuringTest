What is the Imitation Game?

The Imitation Game is another name for the Turing test, created by English computer scientist, Alan Turing. A Turing test evaluates a machine's ability to exhibit human-level intelligence. A classical Turing test involves an interrogator, a responder, and a machine. The interrogator poses questions to the responder and the machine. The interrogator's objective is to determine which response was generated by the machine. A machine that could pass as the responder is said to have "passed" the Turing test. That is, if the interrogator is unable to distinguish between the responses of the machine and the human responder, that machine can be thought of as intelligent.

Alan Turing is widely considered the "father" of artificial intelligence. Turing devised the test as evidence for the proposition that machines can think. Turing argued that thinking itself was difficult to define, and proposed that machine intelligence should be measured in a different way.
